## Summary

Light and fluffy pandan chiffon cake with coconut milk and a fragrant pandan aroma.

## Ingredients

### Pandan juice

- 10 pandan leaves, washed and cut into 2 cm lengths
- 5 to 6 tbsp water, plus 1 to 2 tbsp more if needed for blending

### Batter

- 5 egg yolks
- 45 g caster sugar
- 85 ml coconut milk (KARA recommended)
- 3 tbsp plus 2 tsp vegetable oil
- 1 tbsp pandan juice
- 3/4 to 1 tsp pandan paste
- 100 g cake flour
- 1 tsp baking powder
- 1/4 tsp salt

### Meringue

- 5 egg whites
- 1/2 tsp cream of tartar
- 55 g caster sugar

## Preparation

1. Make pandan juice: blend pandan leaves with water until pulpy. If needed, add 1 to 2 tbsp extra water so the blades can process properly.
2. Press the pulp through a fine metal strainer using the back of a spoon. Measure out 1 tbsp pandan juice for the batter.
3. Preheat the oven to 170 C and place the rack at the lowest position. Prepare a 20 cm chiffon tube pan and do not grease it.
4. Sift cake flour, baking powder, and salt together.
5. In a large bowl, whisk egg yolks and sugar for 3 to 5 minutes until thick and fluffy.
6. Whisk in coconut milk, vegetable oil, pandan juice, and pandan paste until fully combined.
7. Add sifted dry ingredients in 2 additions and whisk gently until smooth, thick, and lump-free.
8. Make meringue: in a dry, grease-free mixer bowl, whisk egg whites with cream of tartar on medium speed.
9. When foamy, add sugar gradually in a steady stream and continue whisking until stiff peaks form, about 5 to 7 minutes.
10. Fold 1/3 of the meringue into the batter until incorporated.
11. Fold in the second 1/3 gently.
12. Fold in the final 1/3 gently until the batter is light and no meringue streaks remain.
13. Pour batter into the chiffon pan from one spot and let it spread naturally.
14. Run a thin spatula through the batter in an S motion to release large air pockets, level the top, and tap the pan lightly a few times.
15. Bake on the lowest rack for 45 to 50 minutes. Do not open the oven door until about 5 minutes before the end to check doneness.
16. The cake is done when a skewer inserted in the center comes out clean. Surface cracks are normal.
17. Immediately invert the pan over a bottle neck and let the cake hang until fully cooled.
18. To release, run an offset spatula around the side, keeping it close to the pan wall.
19. Invert again so the base faces up, gently tap or push the base to loosen, then run the spatula around the base and center funnel to release.
20. Serve chiffon cake upside-down.

## Notes

- Keep all bowls and whisk attachments grease-free for stable meringue.
- Do not grease the chiffon pan; the batter needs grip to climb and rise.
- Leftover pandan juice can be chilled for another bake.
