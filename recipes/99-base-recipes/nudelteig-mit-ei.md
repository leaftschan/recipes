## Summary

Classic Italian egg pasta dough for fresh noodles like tagliatelle, ravioli, or farfalle.

## Ingredients

- 400 g wheat flour (preferably Tipo 00)
- 4 eggs
- 1 pinch salt
- water, drop by drop, only if dough is too dry
- 1 tbsp semola (for dusting while rolling, optional)

## Preparation

1. Put flour on the work surface, form a well, and add eggs plus salt.
2. Beat eggs with a fork and gradually pull in flour from the inside edge.
3. Knead by hand until smooth and homogeneous.
4. If the dough is too firm to knead, add a few drops of water at a time.
5. Keep kneading until the dough is elastic and smooth but not sticky.
6. Shape into a ball, wrap in plastic, and rest for at least 30 minutes.
7. Roll out with a pasta machine or rolling pin, dusting lightly with semola if sticky.
8. Cut or shape as desired.
9. Cook in plenty of boiling salted water until the pasta floats and is al dente, usually 2 to 3 minutes for fresh pasta.

## Notes

- Rule of thumb: 100 g flour plus 1 egg per person.
- Do not leave fresh dough uncovered for long, or it can become brittle.
- For drying, spread shaped pasta so pieces do not touch and let dry fully before storing.
