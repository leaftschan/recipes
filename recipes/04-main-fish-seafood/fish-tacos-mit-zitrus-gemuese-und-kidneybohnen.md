## Summary

Fish tacos with citrus-marinated fish, roasted vegetables, creamy kidney beans, and a fresh cilantro-citrus finish.

## Ingredients

### Fish marinade

- 550 g fish fillet, patted dry and cut into large pieces
- zest of 1 lime
- juice of 1 lime
- juice of 1/2 lemon
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- 1 small garlic clove, very finely chopped or grated
- 1 tsp cumin
- 1 tsp paprika or smoked paprika
- pinch of chili
- 3/4 tsp salt
- black pepper

### Roasted vegetables

- 2 bell peppers, cut into strips
- 1 unripe tomato, cut into wedges or chunks
- cherry tomatoes, halved
- 1 onion, cut into thin wedges (optional)
- 1.5 tbsp olive oil
- salt
- pepper
- pinch of cumin
- pinch of paprika

### Kidney beans

- 1 can kidney beans, rinsed
- 1 tbsp olive oil
- 1 small garlic clove, finely chopped
- juice of 1/2 lime
- pinch of cumin
- salt
- pepper
- 2 to 3 tbsp water

### Citrus-cilantro finish

- juice of 1 lime
- juice of 1/2 lemon
- 1 to 2 tbsp olive oil
- small handful cilantro, finely chopped
- tiny bit of honey or maple syrup (optional)
- salt
- pepper

### To serve

- taco shells, warmed
- extra fresh cilantro
- optional crunchy topping: lettuce, cucumber, or radishes

## Preparation

1. Marinate the fish: whisk marinade ingredients, coat fish, and rest for 15 to 20 minutes. Do not marinate much longer, or the citrus starts curing the fish too strongly.
2. Roast the vegetables: toss bell peppers, unripe tomato, cherry tomatoes, and onion (if using) with olive oil, salt, pepper, cumin, and paprika. Roast at 220 C for 20 to 25 minutes until soft and lightly charred.
3. Prepare the beans: warm kidney beans in a small pan with olive oil, garlic, lime juice, cumin, salt, pepper, and water. Lightly crush some beans with a fork so the mixture is creamy but still textured.
4. Make the citrus-cilantro finish: mix lime juice, lemon juice, olive oil, cilantro, optional honey/maple syrup, salt, and pepper in a small bowl.
5. Cook the fish: pan-fry in a little oil for a few minutes per side until just cooked and lightly golden, then break into large flakes. Alternatively, roast in the oven for 10 to 12 minutes, depending on thickness.
6. Warm taco shells according to package instructions or briefly in the oven.
7. Assemble tacos with warm beans, roasted vegetables, fish, a little citrus-cilantro finish, and fresh cilantro. Add optional crunchy topping at the end.
